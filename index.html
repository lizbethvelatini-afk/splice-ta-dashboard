HRBP Insights
10 data-driven signals · May 6, 2026
▶ 01   Hiring is down sharply — only 6 qualifying hires YTD
Hiring Volume
2026 is tracking to be the lowest hiring year since 2018. With 6 hires (>10d) through May, the full-year pace is roughly 14–16 hires — compared to 27–36 in prior years. Only 3 open reqs are active. HRBPs should confirm with TA whether headcount plans have been deliberately scaled back or if open reqs are stalled.
▶ 02   Time to fill is improving but Senior PM, Growth is critically overdue
Speed
Avg TTF is 62 days. The Senior PM, Growth role has been open 117 days — nearly double the average and a growing risk to the product roadmap. HRBPs should flag this to the hiring manager and TA lead for an escalation conversation. The other 3 open roles are all within or under average.
▶ 03   May had 3 involuntary terminations — all in Engineering and P&C
Terminations
Three employees were terminated on May 15: Alexander Wallace and Jennifer Basalone (Engineering, under Roderick Begbie) and Melissa Colon (People & Culture). Roderick Begbie now has 9 total terminations all-time — the highest of any single manager. HRBPs should monitor this org for morale and span-of-control risk.
▶ 04   Engineering has the highest termination volume of any department
Retention Risk
Engineering accounts for 42 of 121 total terminations (35%) — far above any other department. Of those, 28 are classified as regrettable. With only 26 all-time promotions in Engineering vs. 42 exits, the department has a net talent loss signal. HRBPs embedded in Eng should be conducting regular stay interviews and pulse checks.
▶ 05   YTD turnover is 1.0% — healthier than FY2025's 1.6%, but Q4 2025 was a warning
Turnover Trend
2026 YTD turnover is tracking well at 1.0%. However, Q4 2025 saw 29 terminations and a 3.4% quarterly rate — the highest since Q1 2023. That spike was largely driven by a December RIF/reorg. HRBPs should confirm with leadership whether further restructuring is planned, as employees may still be processing uncertainty from that period.
▶ 06   21 promotions YTD — strongest promotion cycle in company history
Growth
2026 already has 21 promotions through March, making it the highest single-year total on record (previous high was 23 in 2023, full year). This is a strong positive signal for internal mobility and career development culture. HRBPs should leverage this in employer branding and manager conversations as evidence that growth pathways exist.
▶ 07   Liz Velatini is leading 2026 hiring — 4 of 6 qualifying hires
TA Performance
Of the 6 hires (>10d) in 2026, Liz Velatini sourced 4 (Noah Kardos-Fein, Joe Edwards, Bomo Piri, Kristin Wilson). Melissa Colon sourced 1 (Kerri Theriault) and Julian Lenz came via Employee Referral. With Melissa Colon now terminated, TA capacity is reduced. HRBPs should flag any open req coverage gaps to leadership.
▶ 08   Non-regrettable performance terminations are the #1 exit reason
Performance
48 of 121 terminations (40%) are classified as Non-Regrettable: Performance. This is the single largest exit category — more than double the next category (Job Elimination at 17). This suggests performance management is active, but HRBPs should validate that PIPs and coaching conversations are being documented consistently and that managers have adequate support before initiating exits.
▶ 09   Regrettable attrition is concentrated in Engineering — 28 regrettable exits
Regrettable Loss
Engineering has 28 regrettable terminations, the most of any department. Top reasons include "Dissatisfied w/ Employment" (16 all-time) and "Other Employment" (12). This points to compensation competitiveness and manager experience as likely drivers. HRBPs should partner with Comp to run a market benchmarking analysis for senior IC engineering roles, and review engagement scores for Eng specifically.
▶ 10   Product's QoH signal is the strongest — promotions outpace terminations
Quality of Hire
Product has 33 all-time promotions vs. 21 terminations — the only department where internal growth definitively outpaces attrition. Business Development is also healthy (11 promos, 3 involuntary terms). In contrast, People & Culture has only 4 promotions vs. 10 terminations, and Executive has 4 promos vs. 6 involuntary exits. HRBPs in these departments should prioritize development planning and succession conversations.
